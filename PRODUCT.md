# CureRays CRMS

## Register

product

## Users

Radiation oncology physicians, physicists, therapists, virtual assistants, billing staff, and clinic administrators. Many users are older clinical staff working under time pressure who need to review and maintain one patient course without learning where overlapping tools live.

## Product Purpose

CureRays CRMS is a patient-course centered clinical operations workspace. It brings workflow steps, treatment readiness, fraction records, documents, billing evidence, and audit history into one dependable record so staff can identify the next required action and complete it safely.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and clinically dependable. The interface should feel familiar enough to use immediately, dense enough for operational work, and restrained enough that warnings and next actions remain obvious.

## Anti-references

- Decorative dashboard layouts that prioritize cards, gradients, or motion over clinical work.
- Repeated metrics, explanatory copy, and status summaries that force users to scan the same information more than once.
- Oversized patient headers, floating rails, nested scrolling, and controls that obscure the active record.
- Novel interaction patterns, weak contrast, tiny controls, or hidden required information.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the patient course visible while the work changes.
2. Lead with the next review or action, then expose supporting detail.
3. Show each operational fact once in the place where it is most useful.
4. Prefer familiar, full-width work surfaces over decorative containers.
5. Preserve clinical safety, accessibility, and PHI boundaries when simplifying.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Use clear labels, visible focus states, keyboard-operable tabs and modals, sufficient contrast, comfortably sized controls, reduced-motion support, and layouts that remain understandable at narrow widths and browser zoom. Do not rely on color alone for clinical status.
